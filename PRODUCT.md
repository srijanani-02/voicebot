# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Students and hobby developers who want a simple personal assistant they can run locally or deploy with minimal setup. They use it in a browser, often on laptops, to ask short questions by text or voice and get a quick answer back.

## Product Purpose
This project demonstrates a secure decoupled assistant architecture: a React client for capture and playback, and a FastAPI backend that owns all Hugging Face integrations and secrets. Success means the app feels approachable, responds quickly, and is easy for college students to understand and deploy.

## Brand Personality
Clear, modern, calm. The interface should feel lightweight, technically trustworthy, and slightly polished without becoming flashy.

## Anti-references
Do not expose secrets in the browser. Do not look like a cluttered chatbot dashboard. Avoid heavy enterprise admin styling, novelty neon effects, or overcomplicated multi-panel layouts.

## Design Principles
Build around the security boundary first.
Keep the interaction loop obvious: choose a mode, speak or type, get a reply.
Favor concise responses and quick feedback over feature sprawl.
Make deployment and local setup legible for students.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Aim for keyboard-friendly controls, visible focus states, readable contrast, and clear status text for recording, loading, and playback states. Voice should remain optional so the full app works with text only.
